Short version: I mostly agree with Gemini, but its answer is a little too breathlessly positive. The plan is strong and very implementable, but the project will be judged on clean execution, docs, agent-usage transparency, and a working demo more than on architectural ambition.
My outlook: good. This is a scoped full-stack take-home, not a research problem. Alma’s product messaging is about fast, transparent, attorney-led immigration workflows, with a platform that gives visibility and control, so a clean lead intake plus internal attorney workflow maps well to their domain. Their About page also frames Alma as a LegalTech startup combining attorney expertise with AI/ML, which makes the “show how you use agents well” part especially important. (tryalma.com) (tryalma.com)
Gemini is right that the plan covers the assignment: FastAPI, Next.js, persistence, resume upload, auth-guarded internal UI, lead state transition, docs, and agent attribution. The repo structure is sensible, and the NOTES.md requirement is especially important because the prompt explicitly says they are evaluating agent usage.
Where I’d be more nuanced: console email is the right call for the 6-hour window, but call it a “development email backend” or “console email service,” not a full production email integration. In the design doc, explicitly say: for the assessment, email delivery is represented by a swappable EmailService; production would use SMTP/Resend/SendGrid plus retries/outbox/background jobs. That preserves the architecture without wasting time on credentials and network failure.
I would not use Gemini Deep Research for this phase. You already have enough company context and a complete implementation plan. Deep Research would mostly burn time. The best use of time is building, testing, documenting, and recording the E2E flow.
The main risks I’d watch:
Authenticated resume download in the frontend: must use fetch with bearer token and open a blob.
File handling: size limit, extension validation, safe upload path, no raw disk path in API response.
Email verification: tests should confirm two email sends/logs happen.
Docs: RUNNING.md, DESIGN.md, and NOTES.md should be treated as first-class deliverables.
Scope creep: do not add fancy queues, OAuth, Postgres, deployment, or real SMTP unless the core app is already green.
So my actual verdict: this is a solid plan with a high chance of succeeding if implemented tightly. Gemini’s answer is directionally correct; I’d just replace its “perfect / 100%” confidence with “very good, but document the tradeoffs clearly.”
